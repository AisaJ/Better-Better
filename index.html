Fitness , Health Or Both?

Fitness is the ability to perform specific tasks well. Fitness is often very specific to goals or tasks. It involves an activity of some sort that stimulates various systems of the body and maintains a certain condition which is suitable for certain sports/activities.
Health is the absence of disease both mentally as well as physically. Health means a person has good physical health, no injuries, diseases or issues, and good mental health and social well being. For example, An athlete can perform with injuries of have lots of mental issues such as depression or drug dependencies, and still be “fit” but not have health.

Why it Matters
We can’t always choose the state of our health. High cholesterol and blood pressure are largely genetic. Accidents cause injuries that last a lifetime. But fitness, on the other hand, is a choice which can be a way to actively work towards a better health.

" Boxing: as a fitness activity enables the average person to hone some athletic skills, all without having to take a punch. These skills are as follows: strength, speed, agility, hand-eye coordination, endurance, nerve, and power, just to name several."

"Yoga: The purpose of yoga is to build strength, awareness and harmony in both the mind and body. The relaxation techniques incorporated in yoga can lessen chronic pain, such as lower back pain, arthritis, headaches and carpal tunnel syndrome. Yoga can also lower blood pressure and reduce insomnia."


Diet: A healthful diet includes a variety of fruits and vegetables of many colors, whole grains and starches, good fats, and lean proteins. Eating healthfully also means avoiding foods with high amounts of added salt and sugar. Some of the benefits of a proper diet are as follows:

  • Weight Loss
  • Reduced cancer risks
  • Diabetes management...etc

Friendships: Good friends are good for your health. They provide support during bad times, prevent loneliness and give you a chance to offer needed companionship, too. Adults with strong social support have a reduced risk of many significant health problems, including depression, high blood pressure and an unhealthy body mass index (BMI).

Avoid misuse of drugs: Overall, the effects of drug abuse and dependence can be far-reaching. They can impact almost every organ in the human body.
Side effects of drug addiction may include:

  • A weakened immune system
  • Heart conditions ranging from abnormal heart rates to heart attacks
  • Nausea and abdominal pain
  • Seizures, stroke, mental confusion and brain damage
  • Lung disease
  • Problems with memory, attention and decision-making
